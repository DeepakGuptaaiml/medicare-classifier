MEDICARE CLAIMS PROCESSING POLICY
Enterprise Policy Manual — MMSEA Section 111 Compliance
========================================================

1. MEDICARE ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
--------------------------------
An individual is eligible for Medicare benefits if they meet ANY of the following:

  a) Age 65 or older (Primary eligibility)
     - Initial Enrollment Period: 3 months before/after 65th birthday
     - Part A (hospital) typically premium-free with sufficient work credits
     - Part B (medical) requires premium enrollment

  b) End Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) — any age
     - Permanent kidney failure requiring dialysis or transplant
     - Medicare eligibility begins 4th month of dialysis (exceptions apply)

  c) Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS / Lou Gehrig's disease) — any age
     - Medicare eligibility begins immediately upon SSDI approval
     - No 24-month waiting period for ALS

  d) Disability — 24 months of Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI)
     - Qualifying disability per SSA definition
     - 24-month waiting period (waived for ALS and ESRD)

2. MMSEA SECTION 111 OVERVIEW
-----------------------------
The Medicare, Medicaid, and SCHIP Extension Act (MMSEA) Section 111 requires
Responsible Reporting Entities (RREs) to report information about Medicare beneficiaries
who receive settlements, judgments, awards, or have ongoing medical responsibility
from liability, workers' compensation, or no-fault insurance.

WHO MUST REPORT (RREs):
  - Insurers (commercial carriers)
  - Third Party Administrators (TPAs)
  - Self-insured entities (employers, municipalities)
  - Group health plans (when applicable)

WHAT MUST BE REPORTED:
  - ORM: Ongoing Responsibility for Medicals — insurer responsible for ongoing treatment
  - TPOC: Total Payment Obligation to Claimant — settlements, judgments, awards

REPORTING DEADLINES:
  - Initial ORM assumption: within 45 days of assuming responsibility
  - TPOC payments: within 45 days of payment date (or per CMS quarterly schedule)
  - Query response: within 45 days of CMS beneficiary match request

PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - $1,000 per day per claim for failure to report
  - $1,000 per day per claim for reporting untimely/inaccurate data
  - Potential exclusion from federal programs for willful neglect

3. ORM vs TPOC DEFINITIONS
---------------------------
ORM (Ongoing Responsibility for Medicals):
  - Insurer has responsibility to pay for ongoing medical care related to the injury
  - Triggered when medical payments exceed threshold (paid_3 > $750 effective 01/01/2010)
  - Reporting continues until responsibility ends (settlement, claim closure, transfer)

TPOC (Total Payment Obligation to Claimant):
  - Total payment obligation to resolve the claim (settlement, judgment, award)
  - Includes indemnity, structured settlements, and certain medical compromises
  - Triggered when indemnity paid > $0 (effective 10/01/2011)
  - Must report payment amount and date

4. CLAIM SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------
Required fields for Medicare Section 111 claims reporting:
  - Beneficiary: SSN or HICN, name, date of birth, gender
  - RRE: TIN, office code, reporting entity name
  - Incident: date of incident, alleged cause of injury
  - Insurance: policy number, claim number, state of jurisdiction
  - ORM indicator and dates; TPOC amount and date if applicable

Documentation checklist:
  - Proof of Medicare beneficiary status (CMS query response)
  - Payment records supporting TPOC amount
  - Medical records supporting ORM assumption
  - Settlement agreement or court order (for TPOC)
  - RRE authorization and TIN verification

Timelines:
  - Report ORM within 45 days of assuming medical responsibility
  - Report TPOC within 45 days of payment obligation determination
  - Respond to CMS queries within required timeframe

5. FRAUD INDICATORS
-------------------
Watch for the following red flags in Medicare-related claims:
  - Billing for services not rendered or not medically necessary
  - Upcoding procedure codes (billing higher complexity than performed)
  - Duplicate billing across payers or claim lines
  - Unbundling services that should be billed as a single procedure
  - Provider kickbacks or patient referral schemes
  - Misrepresenting injury causation to shift payment to Medicare

Refer suspected fraud to Special Investigations Unit (SIU) and CMS as required.

6. DENIAL REASONS AND APPEAL PROCESS
------------------------------------
Common Medicare denial codes:
  - CO-4: Procedure code inconsistent with modifier
  - CO-16: Claim lacks required information
  - CO-18: Duplicate claim/service
  - CO-29: Time limit for filing has expired
  - CO-50: Non-covered service
  - CO-96: Non-covered charge(s)

Appeal timeline:
  - Redetermination: 120 days from denial date (Level 1)
  - Reconsideration by QIC: 180 days from redetermination (Level 2)
  - Administrative Law Judge (ALJ): 60 days from QIC decision (Level 3)
  - Medicare Appeals Council (DAB): 60 days from ALJ decision (Level 4)
  - Federal District Court: 60 days from DAB decision (Level 5)

7. HIPAA COMPLIANCE
-------------------
Protected Health Information (PHI) handling requirements:
  - Minimum necessary standard: access only PHI required for the task
  - Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) required with all vendors handling PHI
  - Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest for PHI storage
  - Audit logging of all PHI access
  - Breach notification within 60 days of discovery
  - Workforce training annually on HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules

Note: Production RAG/LLM systems must use Azure OpenAI within the tenant boundary
to maintain HIPAA compliance — external LLM APIs must not receive PHI.

8. COORDINATION OF BENEFITS
---------------------------
Medicare as primary vs secondary payer:
  - WC and liability insurance typically pay primary for WC/liability-related treatment
  - Medicare is secondary when WC/liability is primary payer
  - Medicare Conditional Payments: CMS may pay conditionally and seek reimbursement
    when primary payer settlement occurs

Workers Comp Medicare Set-Aside (WCMSA):
  - Portion of settlement allocated for future Medicare-covered medical expenses
  - Required when Medicare beneficiary and settlement closes future medicals
  - CMS review recommended for settlements > threshold amounts

Conditional payments and reimbursement:
  - Medicare may pay claims conditionally when primary payer delays payment
  - Upon TPOC settlement, RRE must reimburse Medicare for conditional payments
  - CMS Recovery Contractor (CRC) pursues recovery on behalf of Medicare
